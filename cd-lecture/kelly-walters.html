Lecture Response 5
Response to: CD Lecture Series: Kelly Walters

Kelly Walters, the founder of Bright Polka Dot, shares not only her art and design research but also her profound commitment to studying the many intricacies that define identity during her presentation. An investigation of the frameworks of values and principles, as well as the common language employed within and surrounding visual depictions of black culture, is central to her practice.

Walters' work is notable for her typeface design, in which she expertly visualizes the portrayal of black women at certain eras of time. She blends and reassembles images and text to create compelling posters that challenge standard conceptions of visual communication. Her creative skills in crafting the interplay between language and archive graphics provide interesting perspectives on visual diversity and design conceptualization. Walters' work compellingly highlights the need of integrating social context and visual aspects.

Throughout the talk, a recurring topic emerges: designers' responsibility to create inclusivity and represent the diverse perspectives that exist in society. Design, as a powerful force, has the potential to shape our reality. As a result, designers must embrace diversity and use their platforms to highlight the views of people who have been historically underrepresented.

Walters' speech is not only motivating, but it also provides important insights into the critical function of representation and innovation in design. Her personal path and experiences teach young designers the value of accepting multiple viewpoints and narratives. Furthermore, her continuous support for diversity and inclusion in the business serves as a powerful reminder of the transformative power of design on society as a whole.
